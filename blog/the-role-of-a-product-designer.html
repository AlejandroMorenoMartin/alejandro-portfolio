Product design

In this article, we will explore the main tasks and responsibilities of a product designer in a typical day-to-day project. Additionally, you will understand the differences between generalists, specialists, and T-shaped designers.

Responsabilities

User research

It involves understanding people and how they interact with your product. Through research, you will learn about the background of your users, their demographic characteristics, motivations, problems, emotions, and goals. Research methods include surveys, observations, and interviews.

Information architecture

It entails deciding how your product is organized and structured. It could be thought of as the skeleton of the product and must be structured in a way that is easily recognizable and explorable by users.

Wireframing

It is a simple representation or design idea of a product. They are often done by hand or with programs like Adobe XD or Figma. They do not involve any complexity in their composition; for the most part, they consist of strokes and lines. They are used to represent ideas and make them visible.

Prototyping

It is a more accurate model or representation of the final product that has functionality. Prototypes can have a physical or digital format and can vary in complexity depending on the company and project. Often, the prototype is made to showcase a specific feature, such as a transition between pages. During the design process, it is very common to create different prototypes.

Visual design

It focuses on how the product or technology being designed looks. As a designer, you need to understand the fundamental principles of visual design to effectively communicate the relationship between functionality and the appearance it adopts for users.

Effective communication

This involves being available to team members through email, presentations, or design and communication programs. Design is a field where collaboration is crucial to achieving goals, so doing it effectively is very important. You need to be a good listener, have a high capacity to receive and handle criticism, and be able to express ideas clearly and simply.

The various roles like product designer

Specialists

They delve into a specific type of design, such as interaction, visual, or animations, and become experts in that field. This type of designer is more common in large companies. Some of their advantages include:

  • Focus on a type of design and enjoy more than others.
  • Have greater knowledge in that field, becoming experts.

Generalist

They have a wide range of responsibilities. Most designers work under this type, and it is very common in companies with fewer designers available. They have some advantages such as:

  • They can expand their skills among different specializations.
  • They can test themselves by working in different areas and thus find their passion.
  • They maintain a fresh passion for what they do, as they perform different tasks instead of dedicating themselves to a single specific thing.

In conclusion, as a designer, there are multiple paths you can take. After landing your first job and gaining experience, you can evaluate which options you like the most and in which fields you want to specialize.
