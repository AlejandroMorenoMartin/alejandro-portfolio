Blog - How to grow as a product designer

Discover the areas you need to improve to advance in your career.

Mar 24

Product design

Firstly, you need to increase the impact of your work on the company's outcomes, whether it’s driving revenue growth or reducing costs.

Secondly, it’s crucial to prioritize task execution, ensuring that tasks requiring immediate resolution receive immediate attention.

Finally, you must be willing to continuously increase your levels of competence and knowledge. In this market, those who stand still are overtaken by those who keep advancing at the pace set by the market. You can rely on courses, books, hiring mentorships, or attending seminars. There are plenty of options available that will allow you to be in a continuous learning mode.

You should improve your knowledge in this areas:

Tools and processes

How to create or maintain documentation, design systems, processes, and tools is important. However, knowing how to identify the problems and needs of all of the above is a much more valuable skill.

Communication

Your ability to communicate design decisions to your colleagues, as well as the ability to communicate with interested parties within the company.

In small companies or startups, you may be in charge of these research activities, while larger companies have specialized designers in this field.

Feedback

You must have the ability to provide feedback in both a positive and negative sense. Feedback should also be received in the same way throughout your career. Therefore, it is important to know how to accommodate the opinions of your colleagues if they are motivated by improvement and you must know how to convey yours from a constructive point of view and for the good of the company.

Stakeholder management

In your company or the project you work for, it may be made up of different parts that must be within your focus. In this way you must know how to transmit information conveniently in a timely manner and know how to communicate with interested parties.

Shipping

The important thing here is to increase the effectiveness of your actions, making your work hours always the most valuable in terms of the company's objectives.

Independence

You must be willing to take on responsibilities and lead projects that may need someone to take charge. If you manage to make your work relevant without anyone having foreseen its completion or without having been properly articulated, you will be demonstrating your capacity for leadership and taking action.

Strategy

To increase your strategic capacity, you must be aligned with the company, with its objectives, its mission, its vision of the future. You must also be clear about the competencies and objectives of the different departments, who they are made up of, and what their relationships are. You must know the product in detail, know how to improve the KPI's. You must know the competitors, the market leaders, know what they do and why they do it. If you have a clear image of all these concepts, it is easier for you to help in business strategy.

Mentorship

It is part of the job to know how to guide colleagues who need it and help them improve their knowledge and abilities. The benefit of this competition is for the company, since better workers imply better performance and results. So always be willing to help and learn how to do it in the best way.

Hiring

Hiring is part of your obligations if you want to grow in your company. You must be in continuous contact with the market, learn to identify talent and know how to offer vacancies that may be attractive to the market.

Culture

Knowing how to transmit business culture and values is very important to be able to advance in your career. You will be the first to advertise your company and make other people get a more accurate image of who works there, with what objectives, etc.

Advocacy

In the same way you must know how to transmit this from the outside. Good advertising in this sense, by employees, guarantees that the company acquires a higher level of attraction in all senses (investment, customers, employability.
