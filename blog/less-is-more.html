Small changes to solve problems

Apr 05

Usability

Facing usability problems

When you face problems in your product, small changes produce better solutions to the problems. When faced with such a situation you must ask yourself, what is the smallest change you could make in order to correct the detected problem.

Often, people on the team will tend to want to eradicate the problem rather than make a quick fix that will allow the user to stop experiencing it. And it is not that eradicating the problem is a bad decision, but rather that in many cases it requires resources and time that are not available and the problem tends to become entrenched.

That is why it is preferable to make a quick solution, that does not require great efforts and that allows that in a few hours or days it is solved, although it is not perfect.

In many cases the problem has been dragging on for a long time and correcting it is inevitably something that will cost effort and time, but small modifications can always be made to solve it or reduce it. This is the best possible option, which also allows you to work on the "perfect solution" afterwards.

Serious usability problems cannot be ignored. It is always possible to provide a solution that can alleviate it, before being able to design and develop something that eradicates it, that is the idea.

Modify rather than redesign

  • Modifications require fewer resources.
  • Can be carried out quickly.
  • Will have more options to be performed.
  • If you redesign, it is likely that problems or situations will appear that were not present before.
  • Both people in the organization and users like the familiar and redesign implies having to learn something new.
  • Redesign involves risky decisions.
  • Redesign involves bringing together different people within the organization.

Make small adjustments

  • It will only require a few tests to prove its effectiveness.
  • You can start by making a very simple and straightforward modification.
  • If it didn't work as you expected, try a more complete modification.
  • If the modification definitely did not work as expected, try a different idea.
  • Look at the consequences of modifications. There are always consequences and some may be unintended. Be vigilant.
