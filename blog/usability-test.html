Blog - Usability test

Some tips to assist you in this task.

Mar 24

Research

Definition

Usability tests can be conducted on a prototype, a website, a mobile application, where users are given specific tasks to complete typical tasks users face in everyday situations. The goal is to understand how users interact with the product and identify any difficulties they encounter in completing the intended flows.

How often?

How to create or maintain documentation, design systems, processes, and tools is important. However, knowing how to identify the problems and needs of all of the above is a much more valuable skill.

How many participants?

A small group of 3 - 5 users is sufficient to identify the main problems of the product and determine how to address them.

How to choose them?

Ideally, some participants should be selected based on the segmentation of the target audience. Others may be users who do not fit into that segmentation. Testing with a "random" user can also be helpful, as simplicity and clarity are appreciated by all. Exposing your product to any user, whether or not they are the target audience, allows for an evaluation of design quality.

Who should observe the test?

This test is enlightening for any team member. Gather as many people as possible (designers, developers, researchers, stakeholders, executives) to observe firsthand how users react. This will enable the group to identify major problems and be prepared to discuss solutions.

What to test and when?

Ideally, tests should be conducted from the beginning and after the product has been launched. Conducting periodic tests throughout the design and development cycle is a valuable tool for making improvements. Tests should allow users to perform everyday tasks that any user would encounter in a normal situation.

What tests should be conducted?

Create lists of tasks or actions you want users to perform. Ensure participants understand what is being asked of them for each task. Provide details or expand task descriptions if necessary.

What happens during the test?

During the test, make the user feel comfortable and relaxed. The focus should primarily be on completing the tasks and clarifying them if necessary, with minimal intervention. At the end, you should have a clear picture of how the test went, which tasks were completed, which were not, reasons for success or failure, notes on user interaction, areas of difficulty, and why. Observing closely and using your ingenuity can provide valuable additional information.
