The brain

The brain has more than 20 billion neurons. It is a very complex machine that we have not yet finished understanding, but we already know some things about it.

Discover in this article, some of the existing scientific evidence and how to use that information to make better designs.

Processing power

Giving the user too much information at once will produce a feeling of overwhelm that can confuse him in his task. Spreading the information into small pieces that can be digested is often the best option and results in greater user satisfaction.

Sometimes it is better if the user has to click more times, if this reduces his need to think and assimilate the information received.

Beliefs

Cognitive dissonance appears when two ideas conflict. It is a feeling that causes us stress, controversy and discomfort and the solution is usually to let go of one of the ideas.

It also happens that in many occasions we prefer to deny information that opposes a belief, instead of modifying the belief based on the new information.

If you want to change your users' minds about something, start by provoking them to make a small change rather than asking them to make a big change or commitment.

Mental models

These are an accumulation of processes, memories and thoughts about how something works that allow us to direct our behavior, allow us to direct our attention and influence the way we solve problems.

We use mental models to try to predict the performance of a new app, software we have never interacted with or a sport we have not yet played.

We must research our audience, so that we can understand what mental models govern them when they encounter or interact with our product.

Conceptual models

These are the representations of mental models about a given product. In design it is very important that the mental model and the conceptual model coincide or else the user will not understand the product well and will tend to reject it.

Creating positive and useful experiences is largely a matter of understanding the mental model of the users and matching it with the conceptual model you are designing.
