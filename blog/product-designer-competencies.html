Blog - Product designer competencies

These are the main competencies that a product designer should have.

Mar 24

Product design

It's important to note that most companies have their own competency frameworks based on the different seniority levels of their designers. Companies aim for the growth of their designers so that the better they are, the more value they can add to the company.

Craft

Many times, we might think that what leads a designer to promotion is based on their technical skills. While these are crucial, they are only part of the reasons that will propel you to the next level.

UI design

  • Typography
  • Color
  • Iconography
  • Layout, visual hierarchy and composition
  • Grid
  • Motion
  • Grid systems

UX design

  • Prototyping
  • User research
  • Design patterns
  • Define probelms
  • Information architecture
  • Accessibility
  • Work with data

Communication

Presenting

In order to convey the reasons behind the decisions made, the designer must be able to communicate these ideas to the rest of the team in an effective manner.

Feedback

To achieve higher quality and more effective results, designers must be capable of giving and receiving feedback.

Collaboration

Here, it's important to know with whom, when, how, and why to collaborate. This is crucial both for building strong relationships and for the effectiveness of day-to-day work.

Stakeholder managment

It's the ability to keep everyone informed while building and maintaining good relationships with the team. It's part of the job to keep everyone updated on the things being done and make this information accessible.

Interpersonal skills

In order to build strong relationships and collaborate with teammates, designers must be fully aware, empathetic, treat others with respect and consideration, and have developed emotional intelligence.

Responsability

Shipping

  • Getting things done
  • Estimations and deadlines
  • Delegating
  • Balancing speed and quality
  • General output

Independence

  • Dealing with ambiguity
  • Initiating work
  • Autonomy

Strategy

Designers must be aware of the company's strategic direction and contribute to achieving the set objectives. Similarly, they should understand the needs and goals, be aware of the business context, stay informed about the market trends, and work to incorporate those realities into their designs.

Mentorship

Companies expect their designers to be a driving force for the people around them. In this way, you should not let any opportunity pass to teach or guide the people you work with and make their work better and more effective.

Leadership

There are many ways in which a designer can develop leadership skills. We can attract talent, be a source of inspiration, organize effectively, solve problems, prioritize and delegate, recognize the work of others, and know how to let go of what is not beneficial.

Community

Having the ability to think and act in line with the values and goals of the company, as well as inspiring this vision in others.

*This is an excerpt taken from the book The Path to Senior Product Designer by Artiom Dashinsky
