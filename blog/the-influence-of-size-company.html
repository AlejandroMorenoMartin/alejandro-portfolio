Advantages and Disadvantages of Business Types.

Mar 23

Product design

Small Businesses and Startups

Startups are new businesses that aim to develop a product and introduce it to the market. Small businesses are privately owned with few employees. In both types, the work carried out by team members has a significant impact on the final product.

Advantages

  • Due to the team's size, you work closely with company managers.
  • There are ample opportunities for growth within these organizations.
  • There is more creative freedom and a greater ability to implement new ideas.
  • The impact of your work is substantial.

Disadvantages

  • There are fewer available mentors for learning.
  • There is a higher level of responsibility, leading to increased stress.
  • Work is done at a faster pace, requiring strong organization and methodical skills.

Large Companies

In these types of companies, it is common to work in teams dedicated to specific projects. Many designers would be thrilled to work for companies that develop some of the most well-known products in the market. Departments in these companies are more compartmentalized and specialized, allowing members to acquire higher levels of expertise.

Advantages

  • There are many people available for learning opportunities.
  • There are broad growth opportunities in different areas.
  • Clear guidelines exist for maintaining uniformity in the creation process.
  • Due to the large number of people involved in projects, you can focus more on specific responsibilities.
  • Over time, you will specialize and master a specific area within the department.

Disadvantages

  • Due to the large number of designers, your work may not have the impact you desire.
  • Creative freedom is limited due to strict guidelines.

Design agencies

Many companies turn to design agencies to hire general design, branding, and digital product creation services. In many aspects, working in these agencies is similar to working in small businesses or startups, except you have different clients and active projects. It is an excellent opportunity to approach the design process from various perspectives, adapting to the specific needs of each project.

Advantages

  • In many cases, you may be the sole designer, and your work will have a significant impact.
  • It is a great opportunity for effective networking with various stakeholders.
  • Clear guidelines exist for maintaining uniformity in the creation process.
  • You have extensive exposure to numerous companies and clients.
  • Great ability to move your resume between different industries and departments.

Disadvantages

  • There are limited references available for learning.
  • Depending on the agency, you may work on the same type of projects constantly.
  • You may not be able to work on a project from start to finish often.
  • Often, your work may not see the light for reasons beyond your control.

Advertising agencies

Multidisciplinary teams in advertising agencies are hired by external clients to build advertising campaigns. It can be a great option if you are interested in learning things beyond the role of a designer exclusively.

Advantages

  • They have greater job autonomy.
  • Opportunity to learn and understand other areas of the business.
  • There is a greater variety of projects available depending on the client.
  • Networking opportunities to collaborate with different brands, clients, and teams.

Disadvantages

  • It is challenging to specialize in this type of agency.
  • You may find yourself working on tasks more related to marketing than design.

Freelancers

Freelancers are self-employed designers hired by companies as independent workers to assist in developing specific parts of projects. They have a lot of creative freedom and flexibility, providing a good opportunity to gain experience and undertake initial projects as a professional.

Advantages

  • They have greater job autonomy.
  • Opportunity to learn and understand other areas of the business.
  • There is a greater variety of projects available depending on the client.
  • Networking opportunities to collaborate with different brands, clients, and teams.

Disadvantages

  • It is challenging to specialize in this type of agency.
  • You may find yourself working on tasks more related to marketing than design.
