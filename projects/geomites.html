Introduction

Geomites is a mobile strategy game available on both iOS and Android platforms. The game's core mechanics involve players managing a variety of characters, each with unique abilities and skills, which can be enhanced with items to improve their statistics.

The project

I am part of a dedicated team working to bring this project to life. As the product designer, my role encompassed the design of the user interfaces, guided by extensive market research into leading games. Players can level up these characters through two different game modes: online multiplayer battles against other players and an offline story mode that offers progressive challenges.

Work methodology

Our team utilized the Agile methodology to ensure a flexible and iterative development process. We structured our work into two-week sprints, each with clearly defined objectives. Throughout the sprint, we collaborated closely, continually testing and iterating on our work to achieve the highest quality results.

Design basics

At this point we reinforce the visual line and the coherence of the project.

Visual style

The visual style of Geomites is characterized by vibrant colors, clean lines, and a cohesive aesthetic that appeals to our target audience. The objective in this point is defining the visual identity of Geomites, ensuring that every element—from characters to environments—was visually consistent and appealing.

Coherence

We established a set of design guidelines to ensure uniformity across all user interfaces and game elements. These guidelines covered typography, color schemes, iconography, and layout structures. By adhering to these standards, we created a seamless user experience that felt intuitive and consistent, reinforcing the overall brand identity of Geomites.

Benchmark

Before I started making decisions affecting product design, I went to the market to see what some of the best companies and products that resemble the product Geomites offers were doing and how they were doing it.

Studying the best

To ensure Geomites stands out in the competitive mobile gaming market, we conducted an extensive benchmark study of some of the most successful games in the industry. By examining these popular titles, we identified key elements that contribute to their success, such as user engagement, visual design, and gameplay mechanics.

Clash royale

Brawl stars

Design patterns

From our benchmark study, we extracted several design patterns that were consistently effective across the top games. For instance, the intuitive and user-friendly interfaces of Clash Royale and Brawl Stars highlighted the importance of clear navigation and accessible controls. Archero's engaging progression system and Shakes and Fidget's unique character customization options inspired us to incorporate similar elements into Geomites.

Design system

To ensure consistency and efficiency in our design process, we created a comprehensive Figma library. This library included all the design components, such as buttons, icons, color palettes, and typography styles. By using a centralized Figma library, we were able to maintain a cohesive look and feel across all game interfaces.

Typography

We chose the Greenze typeface to establish a consistent and visually appealing text style throughout Geomites. This font was selected for its readability and compatibility with our game's adventurous theme.

Color palette

Our color palette consists of ten color families: neutral, red, orange, brown, yellow, turquoise, green, violet, blue, and pink. These colors are carefully selected to evoke the game's adventurous atmosphere and enhance user interface elements.

Icons

We developed a diverse range of custom icons to represent various items, Geomites, age classes, Geomite statistics, and passive abilities resulting from Geomite skills. These icons contribute to intuitive gameplay and enhance the overall visual experience for players.

Components

Our design system includes various components tailored for specific game features, such as leaderboard tables showcasing player positions in different leagues and cards organizing information like mastery points, positions, and game modes.

Sprint by sprint implementation

For the implementation, we utilized Figma for design collaboration and React for development. Each sprint began with a review of design goals and priorities. Designs were created and validated using Figma, with input from team members Marc and Aleix. Once designs were finalized, Marc and Aleix led the development process in React, implementing the designs iteratively.
